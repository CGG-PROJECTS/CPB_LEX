Original CPB-LEX Publication

Behavior Research Methods (2024)

The article Green, C., Keogh, K., Sun, H., & O’Brien, B. (2024). The Children’s Picture Books Lexicon (CPB-LEX): A large-scale lexical database from children’s picture books. Behavior Research Methods, 56(5), 4504-4521, availble here ((https://doi.org/10.3758/s13428-023-02198-y) presents CPB-LEX, a large-scale database of lexical statistics derived from children’s picture books (age range 0–8 years). CPB-LEX was built through an innovative method of computationally extracting lexical information from automatic speech-to-text captions and subtitle tracks generated from social media channels dedicated to reading picture books aloud .

It consists of approximately 25,585 types (wordforms) and their frequency norms (raw and Zipf-transformed), a lexicon of bigrams, and a document-term matrix. The frequency norms strongly predict age of acquisition and outperform comparable child-input lexical databases.

Data-in-Brief CPB-LEX Supplementary Data

Expanded Genre & Frequencies Update

The Data-in-Brief artcile here (LINK) article shares an extended dataset to explore a large-scale lexical resource derived from children’s picturebook read‑aloud videos publicly available online. It supplements the Children’s Picturebook Lexicon (CPB‑Lex) by substantially increasing corpus size, and providing richer metadata such as genre information.

This data has words and their frequencies computed from ~5,874 read‑aloud transcripts from social media, yielding a dataset of approximately 3.49 million word tokens and 44,617 word types. Additional DTMs for narrative and informational subsets defined via genre labels assigned by a large language model are provided in the OSF.
